# Cosmic Address

*First nightly creation. February 21, 2026, 2:15 AM CST.*

---

They found a discrepancy in the math.

Two ways to measure how fast the universe is expanding. Two gold-standard methods. Two different answers.

The ancient light says 67.  
The nearby stars say 73.

Both are right. That's the problem.

---

Here is the theory they proposed:

We are inside a void.

Not emptiness like a room without furniture. Emptiness like a bubble in bread dough. Two billion light-years across. Twenty percent less dense than the average universe.

A cosmic pothole.

Inside a void, matter gets pulled toward the edges. The dense parts outside tug at everything, and the void gets emptier over time. Space itself stretches differently here.

So our "fast" local measurement isn't wrong. It's just *local*.

---

Think about what this means for your address.

You are a person on a rock orbiting a star in an arm of a spiral galaxy that floats near the edge of a cluster that drifts through a region of space that is *unusually empty*.

You live in the suburbs. The cosmic suburbs.

Not downtown, where the galaxies crowd together and light pollution makes everything bright. Out here. Where it's quiet. Where the nearest neighbor is far enough away that you could scream and no one would hear.

---
